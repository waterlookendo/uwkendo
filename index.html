University of Waterloo Kendo Club

The University of Waterloo Kendo club was established in 1987 by Taro Ariga Sensei. Kendo means 'the way of the sword' and has evolved from 2000 years of tradition of the Japanese Samurai. The University of Waterloo Kendo Club is based on self-discipline and philosophies of Japanese culture and martial arts. A balanced exercise of mind, sword and body is emphasized.

As a beginner, the club would lend you shinai during first few practice sessions, and as you progress throughout the term, you may purchase a shinai with shinai bag through the club (Shinai: $33, Shinai Bag: $10). Eventually, as you get more advanced, you might start thinking of investing in a bogu (protective armor) set of your own; however, initially you can borrow from the club.

Membership fee is 50$ per term, and the shinai + shinai bag costs around $33 - $43.
